Hello, world!

My name is Summer Al Hamdani.

Sometimes written as "Al-Hamdani" or "Alhamdani" to avoid confusion; my middle name is not "Al".

I grew up in Fresno, CA as the youngest child of hardworking immigrants, who ingrained in me the importance of receiving an education from a very young age. I received my B.A. in Mathematics (emphasis in applied math) and my B.S. in Biomedical Physics from Fresno State in May of 2019. After taking real and complex analysis, I developed a deeper interest in pure math and decided to earn my M.S. in Mathematics from Fresno State. My Master's thesis was completed under the guidance of Dr. Khang Tran; I studied the zeros of binomial combinations of Chebyshev polynomials (of the second kind), focusing more on the analysis side of analyic number theory. You can read the thesis here, or read a portion of the thesis work as published in the International Journal of Number Theory here. I am also interested in probability theory, harmonic analysis, operator theory, and mathematical biology. Nowadays, I am more of a casual reader/listener of things related to medical physics—most of my interests are in imaging and diagnostic tools.
