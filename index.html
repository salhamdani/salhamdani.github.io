Hello, world!

My name is Summer Al Hamdani.

Sometimes written as "Al-Hamdani" or "Alhamdani" to avoid confusion; my middle name is not "Al".

I grew up in Fresno, CA as the youngest child of hardworking immigrants, who ingrained in me the importance of receiving an education from a very young age. I received my B.A. in Mathematics (emphasis in applied math) and my B.S. in Biomedical Physics from Fresno State in May of 2019. After taking real and complex analysis, I developed a deeper interest in pure math and decided to pursue my M.S. in Mathematics from Fresno State. My Master's thesis was completed under the guidance of Dr. Khang Tran; I studied the zeros of binomial combinations of Chebyshev polynomials (of the second kind).

At risk of sounding overly cliché, I'm interested in a lot of different types of math; (almost) all math is cool math, in my eyes. 😬 Nonetheless, I will usually attend math talks if they are about: analysis, analytic number theory, probability theory, harmonic analysis, operator theory, applied math (especially mathematical biology), and statistics. Nowadays, I am more of a casual reader/listener of things related to medical physics—most of my interests are in imaging and diagnostic tools.

Beyond things that you can otherwise find on my CV (see below), I am proud of my Arab heritage (🇮🇶 🇸🇾) and my roots in the Central Valley of California. I am an ethical vegan and have been since 2017. I was previously vegetarian since 2007. Currently, my poison of choice for killing off braincells is acting as the decision-maker while my homegirl streams Hakuouki: Kyoto Winds on Discord. Not entirely sure how my life has succumbed to this, but I find solace in my husband being amused by my commentary.

The best way to contact me is via email (my full name, without white spaces, at gmail dot com). I will typically respond within 24 ± 12 hours. However, my biggest flaw is that I am a human, and therefore sometimes emails will be buried or I open them but forget to reply in a timely manner. I don't mind a follow-up email after ~1.5 days, depending on how time-sensitive the email is. I choose to keep notifications for social media turned off, so those are (relatively) unreliable ways of reaching me. Sorry!

Portfolio, Publications, & CV


Listed below are all of the data dashboards (created on Tableau) that I built as a Research Assistant in the Office of Institutional Research at Clovis Community College (CCC), maintained on the college's Tableau server.

Student Success Metrics Dashboard

Utilized data from the Cal-PASS Plus Student Success Metrics LaunchBoard to create a dashboard to aid with visualizing time trends of CCC's student outcomes. I combined and cleaned data for different students' Journey Types using Python.

Crush Pantry Dashboard

Crush Pantry is a free food assistance program accessible to all students enrolled at Clovis Community College. The first sheet displays the total number of visits, unduplicated student visit count, and total community impact over time since the Crush Pantry's inception in the fall of 2018. Includes bar charts that dynamically filter the data by demographics. On the second sheet, a visualization breaking down visits by monthly, weekly, or daily visits since inception. Also includes bar charts that dynamically filter data by demographics.

Headcount Over Time

Visualization of the total student headcount (unduplicated student count) over time, with the ability to disaggregate the data based on demographics information (overall, age, gender, ethnicity, enrollment status, first generation status), as well as filter the data based on these criteria. Includes crosstab below for easy reference or download of data.

Historical Course Statistics

Course Statistics lists the top 20 subjects and top 20 courses (as a count or percentage) taken by students at CCC. Filterable by academic year, term, age, gender, ethnicity, enrollment status, first generation status, course location. Includes a crosstab below with the course headcount data. Class Size Distribution Data displays a data table with the average or median number of students enrolled in each course, filterable by academic year, term, subject, course name, and course location. Includes ability to expand/collapse course names and academic terms. Tooltip displays the total number of students, total number of sections, and the total number enrolled in each section of the course.

Spring 2022 Demographics

Breakdown of students enrolled in the Spring 2022 semester by demographics and characteristics. Includes crosstab for easy reference and download of data.

Spring 2022 Statistics

Displays the top 25 courses taken by students enrolled for the Spring 2022 semester by headcount or percentage. Tooltips show a demographics breakdown: overall, age category, gender, ethnicity, first generation status, and economic status. Data filterable by these demographics. Also shows course locations, and part-time vs. full-time enrollment (with count/percentage of students enrolled in a certain number of units displayed in the tooltip).

Student Awards

Time-trend of the number of student awards per year, with the ability to disaggregate by gender, age, ethnicity, first generation status, and economic status. Default is set to degree & certificate recipients per academic year, degrees and certificates (total) awarded per academic year, average number of degrees and certificates awarded per student per year, average number of years to completion, and median number of years to completion. Filterable by division, department, award title, guided pathways, and degree. Dashboard includes a crosstab of the data displayed, as well as a histogram with the number of years taken for students to complete their award(s).

Zipcode and High School Distribution

Map showing the distribution of zipcodes that enrolled students (since accreditation) have lived in, with the list of the top 20 high schools they graduated from and the top 20 zip codes. Filterable by academic year and features the ability to toggle between headcount and percentage.
