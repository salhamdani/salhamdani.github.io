*: Stuff about the thesis

Recalling the geometric series from calculus, we can take the product of the generating functions for the geometric series and the Chebyshev polynomials (a sequence of orthogonal polynomials, denoted Un(z)): this product is the generating function for a new power series, where the n-th term is the sum of the first n Chebyshev polynomials (denoted Hn(z)). If we look at the generating function 1/(1-t)a where 0 < a < 1 instead of the geometric series and multiply that with the generating function of the Chebyshev polynomials, we get the generating function for a different sequence of polynomials, where the terms are the sum of the first n Chebyshev polynomials with binomial-type coefficients. In essence, I proved that these polynomials will have all real zeros most of the time. The specific result is that there exists a constant C (independent of n) such that the number of zeros outside of the interval (-1, 1) for all polynomials in the resulting sequence is bounded by C. Interestingly enough, (-1, 1) is where the zeros of Un(z) and Hn(z) are located. An open problem and a few other possible directions that look fun to pursue were proposed, as well. Several of the tools used in the thesis are among the topics you learn in complex analysis (MATH 128 at Fresno State, MATH 228 if you want to go deeper) and analytic number theory (MATH 216T, if taught by Dr. Khang Tran).
