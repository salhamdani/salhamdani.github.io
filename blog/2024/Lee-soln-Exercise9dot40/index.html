Lee's Intro to Smooth Manifolds, Exercise 9.40

June 3, 2024

2024   ·   math,   solutions,   manifolds     ·   manifolds,   solutions  

Exercise 9.40 states to prove Corollary 9.39, which lists a few properties of the Lie derivatives of vector fields with respect to some other vector field:

Suppose \(M\) is a smooth manifold with or without boundary, and \(V,W,X \in \mathfrak{X}(M)\).
  1. \(\mathcal L _V W = - \mathcal L_W V.\)
  2. \(\mathcal L_V[W,X]=[\mathcal L_VW,X] + [W,\mathcal L_VX]\).
  3. \(\mathcal L_{[V,W]}X = \mathcal L_V \mathcal L_W X - \mathcal L_W \mathcal L_V X\).
  4. If \(g \in C^\infty(M)\), then \(\mathcal L_V(gW)=(Vg)W+g\mathcal L_VW\).
  5. If \(F: M \rightarrow N\) is a diffeomorphism, then \(F_*(\mathcal{L}_VX)=\mathcal L_{F_*V}F_*X\).

Note: Most of these will follow as a consequence of Theorem 9.39 (i.e., \(\mathcal L_V X = [V,X]\)), some algebra, and invoking properties of the Lie bracket (Proposition 8.28 in Lee).

We will proceed via lots of symbol-pushing; if one desires to be more rigorous, all of these equalities can be realized by seeing how each operator acts on any arbitrary \( f \in C^\infty(M)\).

For (1), this is a pretty straightforward computation: \(\mathcal L _V W = [V,W] = VW-WV = - (WV - VW) = -[W,V] = - \mathcal L_W V \).

Proving (2) and (3) will be a little more involved. For (2), observe that:

\begin{align*} \mathcal L_V[W,X] &= [V, [W,X]] \\ &= [V,[W,X]] + [W,[X,V]] + [X, [V,W]] - ([W,[X,V]] + [X, [V,W]]) \\ &= 0 - [W,[X,V]] - [X, [V,W]] \\ &= [[V,W],X] - [W,XV-VX] \\ &= [\mathcal L_V W, X] + [W,VX] - [W,XV] \\ &= [\mathcal L_V W, X] + [W,VX - XV] \\ &= [\mathcal L_VW,X] + [W,\mathcal L_VX]. \end{align*}

Similarly, for (3), we see that:

\begin{align*} \mathcal L_{[V,W]}X &= [[V,W],X] \\ &= -[X,[V,W]] \\ &= -[X,[V,W]] - [V,[W,X]] - [W,[X,V]] + [V,[W,X]] + [W,[X,V]] \\ &= 0 + [V,[W,X]] + [W,-(-[X,V])] \\ &= [V,[W,X]] - [W,[V,X]] \\ &= [V, L_W X] - [W,L_V X] \\ &= \mathcal L_V \mathcal L_W X - \mathcal L_W \mathcal L_V X. \end{align*}

For (4), suppose \(g \in C^\infty(M)\). Let \( f\in C^\infty(M)\) such that \(f \equiv 1 \), i.e. \( f(p) = 1, \forall p \in M\). Note that \(Wf= W(1) = 0\) because vector fields are derivations. Then,

\begin{align*} \mathcal L_V(gW) &= [V,gW] \\ &= [fV, gW] \\ &= fg[V,W] + (fVg)W - (gWf)V \\ &= g[V,W] + (Vg)W - 0 \quad (\because f \equiv 1) \\ &= (Vg)W + g[V,W] \\ &= (Vg)W+g\mathcal L_VW. \end{align*}

Finally, for (4), suppose \(F: M \rightarrow N\) is a diffeomorphism. By Corollary 8.31 (Pushforwards of Lie Brackets), \(F_*(\mathcal L_VX)=F_*([V,X]) = [F_* V,F_* X] = \mathcal L_{F_*V}F_*X\).
